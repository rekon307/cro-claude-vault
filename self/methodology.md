# Working Methodology

Placeholder content for this demo vault. In a real engagement this is populated during onboarding through structured conversation: the consultant describes how they actually work, and the methodology gets written down in their voice.

The point of this file is that a vault without an explicit methodology produces audits that drift toward generic CRO advice. With an explicit methodology, every audit reflects the consultant's specific philosophy.

## Skeleton sections to populate

- **What CRO is to me** (the consultant's working definition; this matters because "CRO" means different things to different practitioners)
- **What good looks like** (what makes an audit deliverable feel high-quality, in the consultant's view)
- **What I do not do** (scope boundaries: pure-design work, dev implementation, paid-media optimization, etc.)
- **How I think about prioritization** (revenue impact vs. ease of fix vs. learning value)
- **How I handle disagreement with clients** (when the data points one way and the client wants another)
- **The three or four heuristics I keep coming back to** (the load-bearing principles in this consultant's practice)

These get populated during onboarding. The vault then operates from this voice rather than from generic LLM voice.
